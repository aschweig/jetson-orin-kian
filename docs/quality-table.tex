Among the models evaluated, \textbf{Qwen3-4B} under llama.cpp achieved a perfect score across all dimensions (0.0 errors, 0.0 chatty, 0.0 poor prose), making it the strongest candidate for on-device deployment. The \textbf{Granite 4.0 Horizon Micro} model matched it on factual accuracy (0.0 errors) with only a slight weakness in prose quality (1.1), while the smaller \textbf{Granite 3.3-2B} kept errors near zero (0.3 avg) with clean, concise output. \textbf{Llama 3.2-3B} proved competitive at 0.8 errors on average with low verbosity (0.3 chatty), though its error ceiling was higher (max 3). Notably, inference engine choice had a pronounced effect: Qwen3-4B scored perfectly under llama.cpp but degraded sharply under Ollama (1.8 errors avg, 0.8 chatty), and Granite 3.3-2B similarly worsened from 0.3 to 1.2 errors. This suggests that quantization format and runtime parameters matter as much as model selection itself.

A clear accuracy--verbosity trade-off emerged across the Granite family. The full-precision \textbf{Granite 4.0 Micro} produced only 1.0 errors on average but scored worst on prose quality (3.0), generating stilted longer-form responses. Its IQ4-quantized variant matched on accuracy while improving prose to 2.0, and the 3B Ollama variant (\textbf{Granite 4-3B}) traded slightly higher error rates (1.1) for markedly better prose (1.4) and modest verbosity (0.2). \textbf{Ministral-3 3B} illustrated the opposite failure mode: reasonable accuracy (1.2) but extreme chattiness (2.1), which is particularly undesirable in a voice interface where excess tokens translate directly to latency. \textbf{Qwen3.5-2B} showed the cost of aggressive size reduction, with errors climbing from 2.0 under llama.cpp to 3.6 under Ollama---the latter hallucinating that ``the fastest fish in water is the dolphin.'' For a child-facing assistant where factual reliability and natural conversational tone are both essential, the results point to Qwen3-4B (llama.cpp) and Granite 4.0 Horizon Micro as the most deployment-ready options among the models tested.
